# WSA App Design Brief

## Product Description

Wall Street Academy is a learning platform for understanding capital markets from first principles.

The app should make students feel like they are sitting with a senior Wall Street mentor and reading a beautifully structured apprenticeship manual.

The design must support concentration.

The design must not distract from learning.

---

# Design Principle

The app is not the star.

The lesson is the star.

Every visual choice should improve reading, understanding, note-taking, or progress.

---

# Theme

Default theme:

Light mode.

Reason:

This is a learning product.
Most long-form study happens better on a clean, bright reading surface.

Dark mode:

Available through a clear toggle.

Reason:

Some students study at night.

---

# Light Mode Palette

Use something close to:

- Background: soft white
- Content surface: warm ivory
- Primary text: charcoal
- Secondary text: cool gray
- Accent: muted gold
- Supporting accent: deep green
- Borders: soft stone / light gray

Do not use loud blue as the main identity color.

Do not use finance-neon green.

---

# Dark Mode Palette

Use something close to:

- Background: deep charcoal
- Content surface: dark slate
- Primary text: warm off-white
- Secondary text: muted gray
- Accent: muted gold
- Borders: soft dark gray

Avoid harsh pure black backgrounds for long-form reading.

---

# Typography

The app should feel editorial.

Recommended direction:

- Serif or refined display font for major headings.
- Clean sans-serif for body text.
- Monospace only for code, file paths, and technical notes.

Body text must be comfortable for long reading.

Use generous line height.

Avoid tiny text.

---

# Layout

## Home Page

Purpose:

Make the student understand what WSA is and start learning.

Sections:

1. Hero
2. What WSA teaches
3. Learning levels
4. Featured first course
5. Philosophy
6. Start button

Keep it simple.

No loud marketing.

---

## Course Dashboard

Purpose:

Show where the student is in the academy.

Must show:

- Current level
- Courses
- Progress
- Next lesson
- Analyst Notebook shortcut

---

## Lesson Page

Purpose:

The core product experience.

Layout:

- Left sidebar: course navigation
- Center: lesson reading column
- Right sidebar: mentor notes, glossary, progress
- Bottom: assignment and notebook

The center reading column should not be too wide.

Ideal reading width:

65–80 characters per line.

---

# Signature Components

## Mentor Note

A calm callout box with practical Wall Street insight.

Example:

"Professionals rarely ask whether the news is good. They ask whether the news is better or worse than expected."

## Mental Model Card

A memorable framing device.

Example:

"Liquidity is oxygen. You only notice it when it disappears."

## Analyst Notebook

A student reflection area saved locally.

Each lesson should allow students to save:

- What I learned
- What surprised me
- What I still do not understand
- How this changes how I see markets

## Assignment Panel

Displays practical lesson exercises.

The student should feel like a junior analyst receiving work from a mentor.

---

# Motion

Use little motion.

Subtle transitions are fine.

Avoid excessive animations.

No bouncing cards.

No flashy page transitions.

---

# Imagery

Avoid generic finance stock images.

No traders yelling on the phone.

No random skyscrapers unless purposeful.

Prefer:

- diagrams
- simple line illustrations
- timelines
- structured cards
- clean educational visuals

---

# Brand Feeling

The site should feel like:

Old Wall Street library meets modern analyst workstation.

Serious but not boring.

Premium but not flashy.

Accessible but not childish.
