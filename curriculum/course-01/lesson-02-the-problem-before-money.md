# The Problem Before Money

## Subtitle

**Why barter could never build civilization**

---

# Big Question

If money never existed, could modern civilization still exist?

---

# Opening Story

Imagine waking up tomorrow in a world where money has disappeared.

No cash.

No bank accounts.

No Apple Pay.

No credit cards.

No Bitcoin.

You walk into a grocery store carrying your laptop.

You want bread.

The cashier says:

> "I don't need a laptop."

You offer your watch.

Still no.

You offer your shoes.

Still no.

You leave hungry.

The problem isn't that you have nothing valuable.

The problem is that the other person doesn't want what you have.

Welcome to the world before money.

---

# Why This Matters

Money wasn't invented because people liked coins.

It was invented because barter eventually became too inefficient.

Understanding barter helps us appreciate why money became one of humanity's greatest inventions.

---

# Plain-English Explanation

Barter is the direct exchange of one good or service for another.

Example:

* Fish for wheat
* Milk for shoes
* Wood for pottery

Simple enough.

Until society grows.

As more people produce different things, finding someone who both has what you want and wants what you have becomes increasingly difficult.

Trade slows.

Growth slows.

Civilization slows.

---

# The Double Coincidence of Wants

Economists call this problem the **Double Coincidence of Wants**.

In plain English:

For trade to happen,

* You must want what I have.
* I must want what you have.

At exactly the same time.

That becomes nearly impossible in a large society.

---

# Mental Model

## Barter Is Like Dating

Imagine trying to find someone who:

* likes you
* is available
* lives nearby
* shares your interests

Finding the perfect match takes time.

Barter works the same way.

Money removes the need for a perfect match.

---

# Wall Street Perspective

Markets exist because exchange became efficient.

Efficient exchange allows:

* specialization
* investment
* economic growth
* larger businesses
* global trade

Without efficient exchange, there is no modern economy.

---

# Historical Case Study

Early farming communities relied heavily on barter.

As settlements grew into towns, barter became increasingly inefficient.

This created demand for a commonly accepted medium of exchange.

Money was the solution.

---

# Modern Example

Imagine trying to run Amazon without money.

Every customer would need to exchange physical goods directly with Amazon.

The logistics would be impossible.

Money allows millions of unrelated transactions to happen every hour.

---

# Common Beginner Mistakes

* Thinking barter only failed because people disagreed on prices.
* Assuming barter works well at scale.
* Believing money was invented by governments.

# Wall Street Minute

Markets grow when exchange becomes easier.

Anything that reduces friction in trade tends to increase economic activity.
