# Money Is Not What You Think

## Big Question

If everyone woke up tomorrow with twice as much money in their bank account, would everyone become twice as rich?

Most people would say yes.

Wall Street would say:

Not necessarily.

That answer is where our journey begins.

---

# Why This Matters

Before you can understand stocks, bonds, interest rates, inflation, banking, or central banks, you need to understand money.

Not the simple version.

Not "money is cash."

The real version.

Money is one of humanity's most powerful inventions.

It allows strangers to cooperate.

It allows work today to become purchasing power tomorrow.

It allows a company to raise capital from people it has never met.

It allows governments to borrow, banks to lend, and markets to function.

But money only works because people believe it works.

That makes money less like a rock and more like a language.

A shared system of trust.

---

# Plain English Explanation

Money is a tool that helps people exchange value.

Without money, trade is difficult.

If you have chickens and want shoes, you need to find someone who:

- has shoes
- wants chickens
- agrees on how many chickens the shoes are worth

That is hard.

Money solves this problem.

You sell chickens for money.

Then you use money to buy shoes.

The money acts as a bridge between two trades.

That bridge changed civilization.

---

# Mental Model

## Money Is Trust You Can Spend

A dollar bill has little value as paper.

A number in your bank account is not useful by itself.

The value comes from the belief that someone else will accept it later.

Money is trust made transferable.

---

# Wall Street Perspective

Beginners think money is something you have.

Professionals think money is something that moves.

They ask:

- Where is money flowing?
- Who has access to credit?
- Who is being forced to sell?
- Who is being forced to buy?
- Is liquidity expanding or shrinking?
- Are interest rates making money easier or harder to obtain?

This is why WSA begins here.

To understand markets, first understand money.
