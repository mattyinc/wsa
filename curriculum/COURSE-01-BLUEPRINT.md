# Course 01

# How Money Moves

## Understanding the Invisible System That Runs the Modern World

---

# Purpose

Everything that comes later in Wall Street Academy depends on this course.

Before someone can understand stocks, bonds, options, valuation, or financial statements, they must first understand money itself.

Most finance education begins with investing.

WSA begins with money.

This course answers a deceptively simple question:

> What is money, really?

That question leads us into banking, credit, debt, inflation, interest rates, liquidity, and central banking.

By the end of this course, the student should no longer see money as pieces of paper or numbers on a screen.

Instead, they should understand money as a system built on trust, incentives, credit, and expectations.

# Course Transformation

Before:

- Money is cash.
- Banks store money.
- Inflation means prices go up.
- Interest rates affect loans.

After:

- Money is trust.
- Banks create purchasing power.
- Inflation changes incentives.
- Interest rates reprice the future.
- Liquidity often matters more than headlines.
- Markets price expectations.
